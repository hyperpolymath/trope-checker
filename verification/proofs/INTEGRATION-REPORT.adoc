*Finding: OPEN-A and B's Target-3 NOT-REACHED are THE SAME underspecification,
seen from the spec-authoring side and the proof side respectively. They are
recorded here as ONE named central open problem, not two unrelated TODOs.*

[cols="1,3",options="header"]
|===
| | Characterisation in its own words

| *OPEN-A (Stream A, spec side)*
| "The split / rejoin / routing operator is referenced but undefined." R-2026-06-23-03

The common root is a *single* underspecification: *the v0.1 calculus is a pure
type-and-grade discipline with no operational (reduction) semantics for its
grade-routing / iterating constructs.* B states this verbatim — "the spec is a type
discipline; reduction beyond β/let is not given" — having had to supply β/let itself
and hitting the wall exactly where new dynamics would begin. A states the same
absence from the other end — the routing operator whose cancellability R-03 governs
"does not yet exist". The two are not merely similar: *`fix`-unfold is a concrete
instance of the very routing dynamics A's OPEN-A names in general* — unfolding
`fix x. e ↝ e[fix x. e / x]` re-applies the body and routes grade across the
fidelity coordinate toward stem:[\{total, unknown\}] (across tiers), and giving it a
tier-preserving reduction is exactly the operational behaviour A flags as undefined.
*One hole.*

[IMPORTANT]
.CENTRAL OPEN PROBLEM (CO-1) — the calculus has no operational model for grade dynamics
====
*The trope-particularity calculus specifies grades statically but defines no
reduction semantics for its grade-routing / iterating constructs beyond the β/let
core.* The one hole has two faces and one additional gate:

. *Spec-authoring face (= A's OPEN-A):* the lossless fractional split / rejoin /
  routing operator whose cancellability R-2026-06-23-03 constrains-in-advance is
  referenced but *undefined* — no typing rule and, more fundamentally, no dynamics.
. *Proof face (= B's Target-3 fixpoint case):* subject reduction through `fix`/lfp is
  *NOT-REACHED* because there is no ↝ rule for `fix` and no observed-retention ρ.
. *Additionally gated by v0.1 O4* (the limit carrier): ordering iterated unbounded
  attenuation beyond stem:[\omega] is the standing `Conat`-vs-tag fork. This is
  *exactly* B's second missing piece, and it is v0.1's pre-existing open obligation —
  so CO-1 is gated by O4, it does not subsume or close it.

*The grade algebra is not at fault.* The lfp fidelity endpoint stem:[\{total,
unknown\}] is already ▷-fixed and tier-stable (`Delta.total_add_Q`,
`Delta.unknown_absorbing`); what is missing is purely the dynamics (1) and the limit
carrier (3).

*Entanglement worth recording (a non-obvious integration result):* v0.1 §7 justifies
the *existence* of the `fix` grade — `lfp(λγ. grade-of(e)[γ])` — by appeal to *L4
monotonicity* of the grade functional. But strict L4 *fails on the fate coordinate*,
machine-proved as `TB.Tier.fate-L4-fails` (≡ `Soundness.fateL4MonotonicityFails`) —
the same F2×F4 non-commutativity phenomenon as the §5 finding. So even the
*grade-side* justification for `fix` rests on a monotonicity now known to be only
*lax*; the lfp-existence argument needs the lax/closure route, not strict L4. This
ties the divergence-adjacent F2×F4 finding directly into CO-1. (The *conclusion* may
yet be salvageable independently of the broken premise — `Bond`/`Merge` remain
monotone (`Soundness.bondComposeMono`/`mergeComposeMono`) and the fidelity endpoint
lands in the ▷-fixed, tier-stable tops stem:[\{total, unknown\}] — but that recovery
is itself unbuilt and is part of CO-1, not a discharge of it.)

== Divergences — adjudicated against the Lean source

=== (2) Core commutativity — *COMPATIBLE, not a real divergence*

*The apparent tension.* Stream A: the cancellative core's composition "reduces to
fidelity addition (`Delta.Q_add`), which is commutative *and* cancellative", and
non-commutativity is "a property of the *full* operation, witnessed by elements
outside the cancellative core (the disagreeing heads `Dropped` and `Falsified`)".
Stream B: it encodes the grade as a non-commutative graded structure with "*no
commutative bundle … instantiated for `Grade`/`Fate`*", "no `CommMonoid` /
Special-Frobenius instance anywhere".

*Verdict: COMPATIBLE.* The two statements live in *different registers* — A speaks of
a *derived algebraic property of specific elements*; B speaks of its *encoding
architecture* (which typeclass/record instances it chose to build). They do not
contradict, and the Lean ground truth settles it decisively. Evidence:

. *The ground truth itself is "a globally non-commutative whole containing
  commutative sub-bundles".* `GradeBoundary.lean` instantiates `CommMonoid Bond`
  (line 221) and `CommMonoid Merge` (line 265) *and* proves the full `Grade`
  non-commutative (`grade_mul_not_comm`, line 340). So "commutative parts inside a
  non-commutative whole" is not a contradiction — it is literally the algebra both
  streams were measured against.
. *A matches the ground truth exactly.* Full operation non-commutative; the core (and,
  derivably, `Bond`/`Merge`) commute. A even names the mechanism: within
  stem:[\mathcal{C}], `atten(Q a) ▷ atten(Q b) = atten(Q (a+b))` is stem:[\mathbb{N}]
  addition — commutative and cancellative.
. *B faithfully ports the same operations, but as bare functions.* `_▷b_` /
  `_▷m_` (`TB.Grade.agda` 213, 285) are plain `Set → Set → Set` clauses, *commented
  "Commutative meet"* (lines 203, 276); the core clause `atten d ▷f atten e =
  atten (d ⊕d e)` with `Q a ⊕d Q b = Q (a + b)` is stem:[\mathbb{N}] addition —
  *derivably commutative*. B simply *declines to instantiate* any `CommMonoid` record,
  precisely so that F2 holds *definitionally* and no proof can lean on commutativity.
. *B nowhere asserts the core (or `Bond`/`Merge`) is non-commutative.* "No commutative
  bundle" is a statement about *typeclass instances B did not construct*, not a claim
  that any sub-operation fails to commute. "X commutes (derivably)" and "I did not
  bundle X's commutativity as an instance" are mutually consistent.
. *Both use the identical non-commutativity witness:* `dropped` / `falsified` (Lean
  `grade_mul_not_comm`; Agda `grade-not-comm` via `gA`/`gB`) — both of which are
  *outside* the cancellative core (`FinFate = present ⊎ atten(Q n)` excludes them).

This is exactly the "*entirely possible*" case: the core commutes as a *derived
property* of a globally-non-commutative structure. *No escalation to the human is
required, and neither artifact was edited.*

== The two B findings — recorded as results, at equal register

Neither of these is a consolation for the unreached fixpoint case (§1). They are
independent metatheorems, kept at a flat register with the gap.

*Finding 1 — symmetric cut is impossible (an F2 metatheorem).*
`TB.Substitution.cut-symmetric-fails` (definitionally `= grade-not-comm`), with
`cut-orientation-matters` exhibiting `g₁ ▷ g₂ ≢ g₂ ▷ g₁` at the witness
`dropped`/`falsified`. The grade-exact value-substitution lemma (`subst`) is therefore
*sharp*: it holds because a value carries ε (a two-sided unit, so non-commutativity is
inert), and it *cannot* be generalised to an orientation-free computational cut. The
predicted F2 wall is delivered as a *theorem about what the structure forbids*, not as
an unproven obligation.

*Finding 2 — tier-preservation is closure, not a homomorphism (an F2×F4 interaction).*
`TB.Tier.fate-tier-meet-hom-fails`: the naïve `tierOf (g ▷ h) ≡ tierOf g ⊓ tierOf h`
is *false* on fate, because `dropped ▷f falsified = dropped` (honest) while
`fateTier dropped ⊓ fateTier falsified = deceptive` — the honest left-absorbing head
`dropped` *shields* a later deceptive `falsified`. This is *why* tier-preservation had
to be stated as set-membership *closure* (`deceptive-absorbs-▷`, `core-closed-▷`) and
why the ⊑/L4 route is unsound (`fate-L4-fails`). It is the same non-commutativity
phenomenon that undercuts §7's lfp-by-L4 justification (§4).
