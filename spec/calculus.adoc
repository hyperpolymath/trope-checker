= The Trope-Particularity Calculus — a normative core
:revnumber: 0.1 (draft for review)
:revdate: 2026-06-22
:toc: macro
:sectnums:
:sectnumlevels: 2
:stem: latexmath

[abstract]
This document specifies the type-level discipline of *trope-particularity*: a
graded effect system over a Turing-complete term language, in which each
operation on a property-instance carries a declared *loss-shape* grade, grades
compose by fixed algebra, and a declared *use-model* yields a decidable verdict.
It pins the loss-shape category, gives the seven effects as typing rules, states
the composition and order laws normatively, fixes the interaction with general
recursion, defines the verdict, states the soundness target with its firewalls,
and defines a language-neutral interchange (the Trope IR) so that a *tropechecker*
can be implemented portably and independently of any one source language.

The key words *MUST*, *MUST NOT*, *SHALL*, *SHOULD*, *MAY*, and *OPTIONAL* are to
be interpreted as in RFC 2119/8174.

toc::[]

== Scope, and a disambiguation that must not be skipped

The word *trope* carries three distinct meanings across this programme, and
conflating them is itself a modelling error this calculus exists to forbid.

[cols="1,3",options="header"]
|===
| Sense | Meaning here

| trope-the-valuation
| The bottleneck (max–min) grading of a path by its weakest link, with the
  argmin locating the edge to repair. (The *invariant-path* sense.) Used below
  only by analogy, for the verdict witness.

| trope-the-residual
| A residual treated as a sum-typed object rather than a scalar error term,
  with a veridicality axis distinguishing faithful from deceptive channels.
  (The *typed-residual* sense.) Reused below as the grade.

| *trope-the-particular*
| An *abstract particular* in the sense of Williams and Campbell: a property
  considered as a non-repeatable, situated instance — *this* quality, here,
  borne by *this* bearer. *This is the object of the present calculus.*
|===

A classical trope is _quality borne by a bearer_. This calculus works on a
*provenance-enriched* trope — a property-instance carrying four fields — and
that enrichment is a deliberate departure from the classical notion, recorded
explicitly so that downstream readers do not attribute it to Williams.

[NOTE]
====
*Trope-particularity* (working definition). The _recoverable individuating
content_ of a property-instance: how much of its being-this-one-in-this-situation
survives an operation. It is *graded and use-relative*, not binary and absolute.
Classical particularity is the limiting case where the only admissible verdicts
are "identical" and "not identical"; this calculus refines that to a lattice.
====

== The object: property-instances

A *property-instance* (hereafter *trope*) is a record over the *field set*

[stem]
++++
\Phi = \{\, \mathsf{quality},\ \mathsf{bearer},\ \mathsf{context},\ \mathsf{record}\,\}.
++++

The four fields carry, respectively: the situated quality itself; the entity it
is a quality _of_; the situation that individuates it; and an honest provenance
of how the record came to be. A *field-presence-indexed* trope type is written
`Trope[S]` for stem:[S \subseteq \Phi]; the fully-present trope is `Trope[Φ]`.
A trope whose quality has been severed from its bearer is a distinct type,
`FloatingQuality`, with no bearer field. Collapse of a quality to a predicate
yields a non-trope type — the predicate's codomain (typically `Bool`).

== The grade: the loss-shape category

A grade records what an operation did to a trope. It is *not a scalar*: two
operations can lose the same _amount_ yet differ in _kind_ and in _honesty_.
A grade stem:[g] is a record of three orthogonal coordinates,

[stem]
++++
g \;=\; (\mathsf{fate},\ \mathsf{bond},\ \mathsf{merge}),
++++

each living in its own lattice. Composition (§4) and the order (§5) act
*componentwise*, which is what keeps the algebra decidable while the term
language stays Turing complete (§7).

=== Coordinate 1 — field fate

A *fate* records, per field, what survived. Let stem:[\delta \in \mathbb{N}\cup\{\infty,\top\}]
be a fidelity element, where finite stem:[\delta] is a quantified loss in the
tropical (min-plus) order, stem:[\infty] is total quantified loss, and
stem:[\top] is loss of *unknown amount*.

[cols="1,3",options="header"]
|===
| Fate | Reading

| `Present`            | The field survives faithfully. (Lattice top.)
| `Attenuated(δ)`      | Survives, fidelity degraded by stem:[\delta]. `Attenuated(0) = Present`.
| `Predicated(P)`      | (`quality` only.) Survives *only* as the value of predicate `P` over it — the checkbox.
| `Dropped`            | Withheld entirely. (Honest bottom.)
| `Falsified`          | *Deceptive dual:* a value asserted faithful that is in fact false. (Lattice bottom; fails every honest floor. Not writable in source — a lowering fault, §6.)
|===

The fate order is a *chain* (R-2026-07-07 (A2); originally a lattice with a
`Predicated ∥ Dropped` antichain):

[stem]
++++
\mathsf{Falsified} \sqsubset \mathsf{Dropped} \sqsubset \mathsf{Predicated}(P)
\sqsubset \mathsf{Attenuated}(\top) \sqsubset \mathsf{Attenuated}(\infty)
\sqsubset \cdots \sqsubset \mathsf{Attenuated}(1) \sqsubset \mathsf{Present}.
++++

`Present` is top; `Attenuated(δ)` decreases as stem:[\delta] increases;
`Predicated(P)` sits below every attenuation and above `Dropped` — a checkbox
retains at least as much of the quality as nothing at all. What the original
incomparability protected is preserved: a use-model needing the full quality
rejects both `Predicated(P)` and `Dropped`; a use-model needing only the
predicate accepts `Predicated(P)` but rejects `Dropped`.

[NOTE]
====
*Ratification record (R-2026-07-07, ADR 0004; analysis in
`spec/notes/2026-07-07-l4-boundary-and-atoms.adoc`).* (A2) `Dropped ⊑
Predicated` dissolves the antichain — one of the two machine-proved sources of
L4 failure — and makes the fate order a complete chain. (A3) the
`Attenuated(0) = Present` identification is *imposed by normalization at IR
ingest* (validators rewrite `Attenuated(Q 0)` to `Present`; composition
preserves normal forms), so carrier datatypes stay as-is.
====

`Predicated` is well-formed *only* on the `quality` field. A grade asserting
`Predicated` on any other field is malformed (§6).

=== Coordinate 2 — bond integrity

The *bond* records the constitutive quality–bearer tie.

[cols="1,3",options="header"]
|===
| Bond | Reading

| `Intact`   | Bearer present, tie holds. (Top.)
| `Withheld` | Bearer field dropped by projection; tie *recoverable in principle*.
| `Severed`  | Tie cut, quality asserted free-standing; *irrecoverable*. (Honest bottom.)
| `Misbound` | *Deceptive dual:* the quality presented as borne by a bearer that is not in fact its bearer. (Lattice bottom; fails every honest floor. Not writable in source — a lowering fault, §6.)
|===

Ordered `Intact ⊐ Withheld ⊐ Severed ⊐ Misbound`. The distinction between `Withheld` and
`Severed` is the whole content of detaching versus projecting-away-the-bearer:
both leave no bearer, but the former is a clean named drop and the latter is a
constitutive severance. *This distinction surfaces in the type, not only the
grade* — detaching changes `Trope[S]` to `FloatingQuality`, so any downstream
operation needing a bearer fails to typecheck.

=== Coordinate 3 — merge (cardinality and honesty)

The *merge* coordinate records whether plurality was collapsed, and whether
honestly.

[cols="1,3",options="header"]
|===
| Merge | Reading

| `Single`    | No merge; one trope in, one out. (Top.)
| `Fused(τ)`  | Two blended into one, *labelled* by provenance `τ`; plurality recoverable from `τ`.
| `Conflated` | Two merged into one, *unlabelled*; plurality irrecoverable. (Bottom.)
|===

Ordered `Single ⊐ Fused(τ) ⊐ Conflated` by recoverable plurality. `Conflated`
is the *deceptive* collapse — the tag that would reveal the merge is discarded,
so downstream is silently wrong about at least one constituent; `Fused(τ)` is
the *faithful* collapse. This is precisely the faithful/deceptive veridicality
axis of the typed-residual framework, instantiated on cardinality.

[IMPORTANT]
====
*`Conflated` is reachable in the grade lattice but not by well-formed terms.*
The lattice is complete — it has its bottom — because the soundness story and the
use-model order require it as a formal element, and because a front end that
lowers some source operation to an untagged merge must have its output *named*
as `Conflated` when the validator rejects it (§6, §9). But no well-formed term
of the source language produces it: see the conflation decision in §6.
====

=== The unit

The unit grade is

[stem]
++++
\varepsilon \;=\; (\,\lambda \varphi.\,\mathsf{Present},\ \ \mathsf{Intact},\ \ \mathsf{Single}\,),
++++

— full presence on every field, intact bond, no merge. `preserve` introduces it.

== Composition and order — the normative laws

=== Composition

Sequencing two effects (apply the second to the residual of the first) composes
grades by stem:[\triangleright], defined componentwise.

*Fate composition* (per field), the partial operation stem:[f_1 \triangleright f_2]:

[source]
----
Present        ▷ f             = f
f              ▷ Present       = f
Attenuated(δ₁) ▷ Attenuated(δ₂)= Attenuated(δ₁ + δ₂)      -- tropical: losses add
Attenuated(_)  ▷ Predicated(P) = Predicated(P)            -- collapse subsumes prior fidelity loss
Attenuated(_)  ▷ Dropped       = Dropped
Predicated(_)  ▷ Predicated(Q) = Predicated(Q)            -- predicate of the bit; rare, defined for totality
Predicated(_)  ▷ Dropped       = Dropped
Dropped        ▷ _             = Dropped                  -- nothing further can be lost from nothing
----

*Bond composition* — meet in `Intact ⊐ Withheld ⊐ Severed`, `Severed` absorbing.
*Merge composition* — meet in `Single ⊐ Fused ⊐ Conflated`, with
`Fused(τ₁) ▷ Fused(τ₂) = Fused(τ₁ ⊗ τ₂)` (nested honest merge, label is the
pair), and `Conflated` absorbing.

The laws stem:[g] must satisfy, to be enforced by the verified core (§8):

[cols="1,4",options="header"]
|===
| ID | Law

| *L1 (associativity)* | stem:[(g_1 \triangleright g_2)\triangleright g_3 = g_1 \triangleright (g_2 \triangleright g_3)].
| *L2 (unit)*          | stem:[\varepsilon \triangleright g = g = g \triangleright \varepsilon].
| *L3 (bottom-absorption)* | In each coordinate, the *deceptive* lattice bottom is a *two-sided zero* of that coordinate's composition (R-2026-07-07 (A1)): `Falsified`, `Misbound`, and `Conflated` absorb from both sides — no honest absorber launders deception. The *honest* bottoms (`Dropped`, `Severed`) absorb from the left *within the honest fragment*, and `Attenuated(⊤)` is absorbing within the fidelity dimension.
| *L4 (monotonicity)*  | stem:[\triangleright] is monotone in each argument w.r.t. the retention order stem:[\sqsubseteq] (§5). Composing after more loss never yields more retention. (*Holds in full on the ratified carrier* — R-2026-07-07 (A1)+(A2) removed the two machine-proved failure points of the original v0.1 carrier, the `Predicated ∥ Dropped` antichain and the `Dropped ▷ Falsified` clause; the §7 least-fixed-point argument is thereby restored. History and proofs: `spec/notes/2026-07-07-l4-boundary-and-atoms.adoc`, `verification/proofs/lean4/grade-boundary/L4Monotonicity.lean`.)
| *L5 (conflation infectiousness)* | Once the merge coordinate is `Conflated`, no composition restores `Fused` or `Single`. (A corollary of L3, stated separately because it is the non-compositionality-of-deceptive-channels result and the moral core.)
|===

[NOTE]
====
L1–L2 make stem:[(\text{grades}, \triangleright, \varepsilon)] a monoid, hence
the effect system is a *graded monad* on the term category with this monoid as
its grade object. L4 makes the order a *lax* compatibility, which is what the
verdict subsumption (§5, §6) needs. None of L1–L5 is novel as a _shape_; the
content is that the three-coordinate loss-shape monoid is the right grade object
for trope-particularity, which §10-O1 marks as the open completeness question.
====

=== The retention order and use-models

Define stem:[\sqsubseteq] as the *retention order*: stem:[x \sqsubseteq y] means
"stem:[y] retains at least as much particularity as stem:[x]" — i.e. stem:[y] is
higher (less lossy) in every coordinate lattice. stem:[\sqsubseteq] is the
componentwise product order; it is decidable because each coordinate lattice has
decidable order (finite, except the fidelity dimension stem:[\mathbb{N}\cup\{\infty,\top\}],
which is a decidable linear order with two adjoined tops).

A *use-model* stem:[U] declares a *floor* stem:[\mathrm{floor}(U)] — the minimum
retained particularity the use requires, possibly specified per field. The floor
is data the author *declares*; it is the only place a use enters the calculus.

== The seven effects as typing rules

The typing judgement is

[stem]
++++
\Gamma \vdash e : A \mathbin{!} g
++++

— "stem:[e] produces an stem:[A] with trope-effect grade stem:[g]." Function
types carry latent grade: stem:[A \xrightarrow{g} B] is "applying produces loss
stem:[g]." The structural rules (variable, abstraction, application, `let`,
`fix`) are standard; `let` is where composition enters and `fix` is in §7. The
six *writable* effects and the subsumption rule follow. (The seventh,
conflation, is treated in §6.1.)

.T-Preserve
[%hardbreaks]
....
Γ ⊢ e : Trope[S] ! g
──────────────────────────────────
Γ ⊢ preserve e : Trope[S] ! g ▷ ε
....

.T-Project — drop a named field-set, keep S
[%hardbreaks]
....
Γ ⊢ e : Trope[S′] ! g     S ⊆ S′
───────────────────────────────────────────────
Γ ⊢ project[S] e : Trope[S] ! g ▷ drop(S′ ∖ S)
....

where `drop(D)` is the grade with `fate(φ)=Dropped` for `φ∈D`, `Present`
otherwise; `bond = Withheld` if `bearer∈D` else `Intact`; `merge = Single`.

.T-Collapse — quality becomes a predicate; everything else withheld
[%hardbreaks]
....
Γ ⊢ e : Trope[S] ! g     quality ∈ S     P : Quality → C
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Γ ⊢ collapse[P] e : C ! g ▷ predicate(P)
....

where `predicate(P)` has `fate(quality)=Predicated(P)`, all other fields
`Dropped`, `bond = Withheld`, `merge = Single`.

.T-Detach — sever the quality–bearer bond; quality survives intact
[%hardbreaks]
....
Γ ⊢ e : Trope[S] ! g     {quality, bearer} ⊆ S
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Γ ⊢ detach e : FloatingQuality ! g ▷ sever
....

where `sever` has `fate(quality)=Present`, `fate(bearer)=fate(context)=
fate(record)=Dropped`, `bond = Severed`, `merge = Single`. The `Severed`
bond — not the dropped fields — is what distinguishes detaching from a
projection that drops the same fields; and the type change to `FloatingQuality`
makes the severance structurally visible.

.T-Attenuate — degrade fidelity by δ (δ = ⊤ when the amount is unknown)
[%hardbreaks]
....
Γ ⊢ e : A ! g
────────────────────────────────────────
Γ ⊢ attenuate[δ] e : A ! g ▷ atten(δ)
....

where `atten(δ)` sets `fate(φ)=Attenuated(δ)` for every field present in `A`,
leaves `bond` and `merge` unchanged.

.T-Fuse — two into one, labelled by provenance τ
[%hardbreaks]
....
Γ ⊢ e₁ : A ! g₁     Γ ⊢ e₂ : A ! g₂
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Γ ⊢ fuse[τ] e₁ e₂ : A ! (g₁ ⊔ g₂) ▷ label(τ)
....

where `label(τ)` sets `merge = Fused(τ)`, fates `Present` (the labelled blend is
faithful), `bond` the meet of the inputs'. The `⊔` joins the two input grades
(the fused value is no better than its worse constituent in any coordinate). The
tag `τ` is *required*: a fusion term with no tag does not parse (§6.1).

.T-Sub — conservative weakening: you MAY always claim more loss than occurred
[%hardbreaks]
....
Γ ⊢ e : A ! g     g′ ⊑ g
────────────────────────────
Γ ⊢ e : A ! g′
....

[NOTE]
====
T-Sub weakens *downward* in retention only. Claiming *less* loss than occurred is
unsound and has no rule. This one-sidedness is what makes the verdict (§6) a
safe over-approximation.
====

== The conflation decision, and the verdict

=== Decision: conflation is not a source primitive

[cols="1,4",options="header"]
|===
| ID | Decision (dated, for the decision-log)

| *R-2026-06-22* | Conflation SHALL NOT be a writable primitive. `fuse[τ]` *requires* a tag; an untagged merge is a syntax error and does not elaborate. `Conflated` exists only as the absorbing bottom of the merge coordinate (§3.3); a well-formed term never produces it. Conflation therefore arises *only* as a *lowering fault*: when a front end lowers a source operation to a merge and fails to supply a tag, the resulting IR is malformed and the validator (§9) rejects it, *naming* the offending node's merge as `Conflated`. The tropechecker is, operationally, a *conflation detector at the lowering boundary*.
|===

This is the door-too-small reading: the dangerous state exists in the semantics
but is structurally unreachable by well-formed terms. It makes the moral content
— _do not silently merge particulars_ — a validation error rather than a
guideline.

[IMPORTANT]
.Normative variant (conflation as a verdict-boundary primitive)
====
Implementations MAY instead admit conflation as a writable primitive caught at
the verdict rather than at validation. Exactly three things change, and nothing
else:

. *Add* a rule `T-Conflate : Γ ⊢ e₁ : A ! g₁,  Γ ⊢ e₂ : A ! g₂  ⟹  Γ ⊢ conflate e₁ e₂ : A ! (g₁ ⊔ g₂) ▷ unlabel`, where `unlabel` sets `merge = Conflated`.
. *Relax* T-Fuse so an untagged merge is well-formed and elaborates to `conflate`.
. *Move* the conflation check from the validation boundary (§9) to the verdict (§6.2): a `Conflated` grade is then rejected only when `floor(U)` demands `merge ⊒ Fused`.

The recommended reading (R-2026-06-22) is _prevent_; the variant is _detect_.
An implementation MUST pick one and state which in its conformance profile.
====

=== The verdict

The verdict is *not* a typing rule; it is the top-level checking judgement.
Given a closed program with final grade stem:[g] and a use-model stem:[U]:

[stem]
++++
\mathsf{p\text{-}sufficient}(g, U) \;\iff\; \mathrm{floor}(U) \sqsubseteq g.
++++

If the check fails, the verdict is `p-insufficient`, and the report MUST carry a
*witness*: the operation (DAG edge, §9) whose composition first pushed the
running grade below stem:[\mathrm{floor}(U)] in some coordinate. This witness is
the trope-particularity analogue of the invariant-path argmin — it names the
edge to repair, not merely that repair is needed.

[NOTE]
====
The verdict enters the use-model as a *subsumption against a declared floor*,
which is what keeps the whole construction a type discipline rather than a
runtime monitor. Read dually: effects *produce* graded residuals (a graded
monad), use-models *demand* graded residuals (a graded comonad / coeffect), and
"is the leftover enough" is the comparison of a produced grade against a demanded
grade in one graded structure. That the producing monad and the demanding
comonad form an adjunction whose unit/counit are exactly produce/demand is a
*conjecture* (§10-O3), not established here.
====

== General recursion, and why it does not break the checker

`fix` is what makes the term language Turing complete. Its grade rule takes the
*least fixed-point grade* of the body's grade functional:

[%hardbreaks]
....
Γ, x : (A ─g→ B) ⊢ e : (A ─g→ B)     g = lfp(λγ. grade-of(e)[γ])
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Γ ⊢ fix x. e : (A ─g→ B) ! ε
....

The least fixed point exists because each coordinate lattice is complete and, by
*L4*, the grade functional is monotone. A recursive trope-transformer that can
attenuate without bound has fidelity grade stem:[\top] — and that is *correct*,
not a failure: an unbounded loop of lossy steps yields an unknown residual, the
checker reports stem:[\top], and any use-model demanding better than stem:[\top]
rejects it.

[IMPORTANT]
====
*Turing-completeness does not break the checker; it means some grades are
stem:[\top], and stem:[\top] is an honest answer.* The checker stays total
because it computes on the grade lattice, never on the term. The undecidability
that Rice's theorem guarantees is discharged honestly into the stem:[\top]
element, exactly as the Aletheia design discharges it into an honest "cannot
guarantee."
====

*Inference versus checking.* Synthesising the minimal grade of an arbitrary
fixpoint is undecidable. The checker SHALL therefore be *bidirectional*: grades
are *checked* against *declared* loss-signatures at every recursive and effectful
boundary (the author annotates the loss signature of a recursive
trope-transformer, as one annotates an effect signature), and *inferred* only
through the straight-line, non-recursive fragment (decidable, convenient).
Declared signatures at the boundaries; inference in between.

== Soundness, and the firewalls

Let stem:[\rho(\text{in}, \text{out})] be the *observed retention* of one
execution: the grade read off by comparing the input and output trope records.
The verified core (§9) targets:

[cols="1,4",options="header"]
|===
| ID | Statement

| *Grade soundness* | If stem:[\Gamma \vdash e : A \mathbin{!} g] then for every well-typed input, stem:[g \sqsubseteq \rho(\text{in}, \text{out})]: the declared grade never claims more retention than the execution delivers (equivalently, it over-approximates loss).
| *Verdict soundness* (corollary) | If the checker reports `p-sufficient` (i.e. stem:[\mathrm{floor}(U)\sqsubseteq g]) then by transitivity stem:[\mathrm{floor}(U)\sqsubseteq \rho], so the real residual meets the use-model.
|===

[WARNING]
.Two firewalls — state both, do not let the theorem launder them
====
. *Soundness, not completeness.* The checker MAY be conservative: it MAY report
`p-insufficient` where the true residual would in fact have sufficed. This is the
safe direction and is acceptable. No completeness is claimed.

. *Relative to faithful lowering.* Grade soundness bounds *IR-level* loss by
*IR-level* grades. It does *not* prove that a front end's claim "this source
operation _is_ a `collapse[P]`" is true. *Lowering correctness is a separate,
per-front-end proof obligation* (§10-O2) and is NOT discharged by this theorem.
This is the same firewall as "the analogy is not an instance": the core's
guarantee is conditional on faithful lowering, and that conditionality lives in
the spec, in the open, not hidden in a footnote.
====

== The portable tropechecker

The tropechecker MUST depend on no source language's syntax or semantics. It
depends only on a language-neutral interchange.

=== The Trope IR

The portable artifact is a *Trope IR*: a labelled DAG.

* *Nodes* are property-instances, each carrying its field-presence over
stem:[\Phi] and its type tag (`Trope[S]`, `FloatingQuality`, or a collapse
codomain).
* *Edges* are effect applications, each carrying its loss-shape grade (§3).
* A distinguished *use-model node* carries stem:[\mathrm{floor}(U)].

The IR's *canonical wire format SHALL be a JSON-schema-validated document*
(`schemas/trope-ir.schema.json`); an AsciiDoc normative spec accompanies it; an
*OPTIONAL* Nickel authoring contract MAY be offered front-side. (This mirrors,
deliberately and verbatim, the invariant-path I/O-contract decision:
author-side contracts are courtesy, generated from the schema; the schema is the
trust boundary.) The grade algebra — stem:[\triangleright], stem:[\sqsubseteq],
and stem:[\mathrm{floor}] — SHALL be specified *in the IR spec itself*,
language-neutrally, so that two independent checker implementations agree by
construction.

=== The checker as a pure function

The tropechecker is a *pure function from Trope IR to a verdict object*:
`p-sufficient` / `p-insufficient`, plus the witness edge (§6.2). It performs
grade composition over the DAG and the final floor-subsumption. *It MUST NOT
parse, execute, or otherwise depend on source.* That independence is what makes
it portable: it is a checker *of the IR*, and the IR is the contract.

[cols="1,3",options="header"]
|===
| Property | Consequence

| *Independence* | Own repository, own versioned IR schema, own conformance suite. The Turing-complete language is the *first* front end that lowers to the IR — dogfooding without coupling.
| *Multi-producer* | A static analyser for an *existing* language MAY emit Trope IR for code it did not author (e.g. lowering a dataframe pipeline's operations to `project` / `collapse` / `fuse` edges). The same checker then trope-checks it.
| *Honest trust boundary* | Front-end grade annotations are *claims*. The checker validates the *composition* of claims; it cannot validate that a source operation truly *is* a given effect. That is lowering correctness (§10-O2), per front end.
|===

=== Verified core, fast core, conformance suite

The *reference* tropechecker SHOULD be an Agda development that *defines* the
loss-shape category, *proves* L1–L5 and the decidability of stem:[\sqsubseteq],
and *proves* grade soundness (§8). That Agda artifact is the portable
*semantics*. The *shipping* checker (Rust is the recommended target, continuing
the shape-IR lineage, for speed) is validated against the Agda reference by a
*conformance suite*: a corpus of `IR-fragment → expected-verdict` pairs that any
implementation MUST pass. The particularity trainer is, in effect, a *human*
conformance suite for the taxonomy; the machine suite is its formal sibling.
"Portable" thereby means *means-the-same-everywhere* — because the meaning is a
checked Agda artifact and the suite enforces agreement — not merely
*runs-everywhere*.

== Open obligations and falsifiers

[cols="1,4",options="header"]
|===

| *O1 (completeness of the seven)* | The claim that the writable effects are _exhaustive_ is, at present, a well-chosen list, not a theorem. *Falsifier:* exhibit a particularity-affecting operation that is none of the six writable forms (plus the conflation fault). *Upgrade path:* if the three axes — loss-structure × bond-integrity × cardinality/honesty — are the true generators, *derive* the typology as the inhabited cells of their product and *prove* exhaustiveness relative to those axes, turning the list into a theorem (the same move that turned "three layers in a pipeline" into "one substrate, two analyses, one law"). Graded monads, coeffects, and effect rows are established machinery

a graded effect discipline tracking *which kind* of particularity-loss an
operation inflicts on a provenance-enriched metaphysical trope, with

use-model coeffect demand, lowered to a language-neutral IR — is, as a *named
combination*, the contribution. The atoms are mostly already named; the
unclaimed ground is the unification and the three-coordinate loss-shape grade.

calculus agree, which is the point.
