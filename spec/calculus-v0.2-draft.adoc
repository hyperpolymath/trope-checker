[abstract]
This is a *draft addendum* to the trope-particularity calculus v0.1
(`spec/calculus.adoc`, `:revnumber: 0.1`). It records, as dated normative
obligations, four facts about the grade algebra that are now *machine-proved* in
the Lean 4 + Mathlib development at `verification/proofs/lean4/grade-boundary/`
(sorry-free; `#print axioms` = `propext`/`Quot.sound` only). The work here is
*documentation only*: the algebra is already established; this document lifts it
into normative spec text and binds each new obligation to its governing theorem.
It does *not* edit v0.1 — it is written as a separate file precisely so the merge
step can diff it against v0.1 — and it neither re-derives the theorems nor reopens
the proved algebra; it binds each fact to its Lean name. v0.1's open obligation
*O1* (completeness of the seven
effects) is untouched and remains open throughout.

The key words *MUST*, *MUST NOT*, *SHALL*, *SHALL NOT*, *SHOULD*, *MAY*, and
*OPTIONAL* are to be interpreted as in RFC 2119/8174.

== Provenance, and what is being recorded (invent nothing)

The four obligations below are *not* new claims. Each is a lift, in substance, of a
green theorem from the grade-boundary harness, which is itself the single source
of truth for the algebra. The carrier the theorems range over is, exactly:

* fidelity `Delta = WithTop (WithTop ℕ)` (`dplus = (+)`, unit `Q 0 = 0`);
* `Fate = Present | Atten Delta | Predicated | Dropped | Falsified`;
* `Bond = Intact | Withheld | Severed | Misbound`;
* `Merge = Single | Fused | Conflated`;
* `Grade` = the flat record `Fate⁴ × Bond × Merge`, with *independent* fields.

[IMPORTANT]
.Two firewalls carried forward in substance from the harness, not to be laundered
====
. *No Frobenius / cancellative / conical framing exists in v0.1.* A grep of
`calculus.adoc` finds none (its only near-hit, *O3*, is the unrelated
produce/demand *adjunction* conjecture). The obligations below are therefore *new
recordings of proved structure*, not transcriptions of existing v0.1 prose.

. *"CF" (cancellative + conical ⇒ a lossless special-Frobenius split/merge) is
refuted as universal*, and this document MUST NOT be read as reinstating it. It is
ruled out twice over: by left-absorption of the deceptive bottoms
(R-2026-06-23-03, *L5*, `grade_not_leftCancel`) and, independently, by
non-commutativity of the full operation (R-2026-06-23-01, `grade_mul_not_comm`).
What survives CF's refutation is *cancellation on the finite-fidelity core*
(R-2026-06-23-03) — that algebraic fact alone, and no splitting or merging law,
global or core-local.
====

== R-2026-06-23-01 — the grade is a *non-commutative* product monoid

[cols="1,4",options="header"]
|===
| ID | Obligation (normative)

| *R-2026-06-23-01* | The grade carrier under composition stem:[\triangleright] is
a *monoid*, formed as the *componentwise product* of its coordinate monoids
(`Fate⁴ × Bond × Merge`); this is the *degenerate-to-product* structure, confirmed
constructively (`instMonoid`). That product *MUST NOT* be assumed commutative: it
is *non-commutative* (`grade_mul_not_comm`). Consequently any algebraic model of
the operation stem:[\triangleright] *MUST* treat it as order-sensitive, and
*SHALL NOT* assume `CommMonoid`, `AddCommMonoid`, or special-Frobenius structure
for it. The non-commutativity is a property of the *full* operation, witnessed by
elements outside the cancellative core (the disagreeing heads `Dropped` and
`Falsified`); special-Frobenius is moreover unavailable across the honest region,
whose non-cancellative honest-but-lossy tier obstructs its comonoid leg
(R-2026-06-23-03).
|===

This records v0.1 *L1–L2* (monoid) at full strength while adding the order
discipline v0.1 left implicit. The witness to non-commutativity is intrinsic to
the `Fate` coordinate: it carries *two* distinct left-absorbing heads, `Dropped`
and `Falsified`, which disagree (`Dropped ▷ Falsified = Dropped` but
`Falsified ▷ Dropped = Falsified`), so swapping operands changes the result
(`Fate.not_comm`, lifted to the `fQuality` field by `grade_mul_not_comm`).

[IMPORTANT]
====
*Order matters, not merely grade-sum.* Because stem:[\triangleright] is
non-commutative, two effect sequences with the *same multiset* of per-step grades
*MAY* compose to *different* total grades. A checker, an IR producer, or any
optimiser *MUST NOT* reorder effect edges (v0.1 §9) under the assumption that
composition is symmetric. This is the precise sense in which special-Frobenius —
which presupposes a commutative comonoid/monoid interaction — is *unavailable*:
the obstruction is structural (order-sensitivity), independent of, and additional
to, the cancellation obstruction of R-2026-06-23-03.
====

== R-2026-06-23-02 — conicality of the full grade

[cols="1,4",options="header"]
|===
| ID | Obligation (normative)

| *R-2026-06-23-02* | The full grade carrier is *conical*: for all grades
stem:[a, b], if stem:[a \triangleright b = \varepsilon] then stem:[a = \varepsilon]
*and* stem:[b = \varepsilon] (`grade_conical`). The unit grade stem:[\varepsilon]
(full presence, intact bond, single merge) *MUST NOT* be treated as reachable
under composition by any pair other than stem:[\varepsilon \triangleright \varepsilon].
|===

Conicality holds on the *entire* carrier (not only the core), and is proved
fieldwise from per-coordinate conicality (`Fate.conical`, `Bond.conical`,
`Merge.conical`). Operationally: no amount of loss can be *manufactured from* the
unit, and the loss-free state cannot be *re-attained* except from itself. A
verdict engine *MAY* rely on this — a final grade of stem:[\varepsilon]
witnesses a genuinely loss-free pipeline, never an accidental cancellation of
losses against gains, because there are no gains.

== R-2026-06-23-03 — the three-tier cancellation boundary (headline)

[cols="1,4",options="header"]
|===
| ID | Obligation (normative)

| *R-2026-06-23-03* | The carrier is stratified by cancellability into *three
strict tiers*. Write stem:[\mathcal{C}] for the *cancellative core*,
stem:[\mathcal{H}] for the *honest* region, and stem:[\mathcal{G}] for the *full*
carrier. Then the inclusions are *strict*:
stem:[\mathcal{C} \subsetneq \mathcal{H} \subsetneq \mathcal{G}]. *Cancellation
holds ONLY on the finite-fidelity core stem:[\mathcal{C}]*
(`honest_fidelity_cancel`), and *fails everywhere above it* — on the
honest-but-lossy tier and on the deceptive tier alike; the full carrier is
*non-left-cancellative* (`grade_not_leftCancel`). Accordingly, *should* a future
revision introduce a lossless fractional split, rejoin, or routing operator (none
exists today — see OPEN-A), that operator *MUST* be licensed *ONLY* on
stem:[\mathcal{C}], the sole region carrying the cancellation it would rely on;
on stem:[\mathcal{H} \setminus \mathcal{C}] and on
stem:[\mathcal{G} \setminus \mathcal{H}] it *MUST* be treated as lossy and
irreversible, and *MUST NOT* be presented as recoverable.
|===

The three tiers, by their inhabitants:

[cols="1,1,3",options="header"]
|===
| Tier | Region | Inhabitants and cancellation status

| *1* | *Cancellative core* stem:[\mathcal{C}]
| The finite-fidelity region — `Atten(Q n)` with stem:[n \in \mathbb{N}], where
  `Delta.Q` embeds stem:[(\mathbb{N}, +, 0)] injectively and additively — together
  with the coordinate units (`Present`, `Intact`, `Single`). *Cancellation holds*
  here and *only* here (`honest_fidelity_cancel`).

| *2* | *Honest-but-lossy* stem:[\mathcal{H} \setminus \mathcal{C}]
| `Dropped`, `Predicated`, `Severed`, `Withheld`, `Fused`, and `Total` (honest
  stem:[\infty]). These are *honest* — they tell no lie about what was lost — yet
  they are *non-cancellative* (`Fate.predicated_honest_not_cancel`,
  `Bond.severed_honest_not_cancel`, `fidelity_total_honest_not_cancel`; `Withheld`
  and `Fused` belong here by the carrier's order structure but carry no individual
  witness yet — OPEN-C). Honest loss is still loss: it cannot be undone.

| *3* | *Deceptive, absorbing bottoms* stem:[\mathcal{G} \setminus \mathcal{H}]
| `Falsified` (fate), `Misbound` (bond), `Conflated` (merge), and `Unknown`
  (fidelity). Each is a *left zero* (`Fate.falsified_absorbing`,
  `Bond.misbound_absorbing`, `Merge.conflated_absorbing`,
  `Delta.unknown_absorbing`): it absorbs everything composed after it, so it
  cannot be cancelled back out. This is *L5*.
|===

[WARNING]
.Two strict inclusions, two distinct witnesses — do not collapse them
====
The boundary is *three-tier, not two-tier*: the non-cancellative set is
*strictly larger* than the deceptive set.

. stem:[\mathcal{C} \subsetneq \mathcal{H}] is witnessed by an *honest* element
that nonetheless fails to cancel — e.g. `Total`, `Severed`, or `Predicated`
(`fidelity_total_honest_not_cancel`, `Bond.severed_honest_not_cancel`,
`Fate.predicated_honest_not_cancel`). "Honest" does *not* imply "cancellative".

. stem:[\mathcal{H} \subsetneq \mathcal{G}] is witnessed by a *deceptive* element
— e.g. `Falsified` — at which left-cancellation fails outright
(`grade_not_leftCancel`).

Conflating these two would mis-state the algebra: it would either (wrongly) license
splitting on honest-but-lossy elements, or (wrongly) brand honest loss as
deception. Both are forbidden.
====

[IMPORTANT]
.The split / rejoin / routing constraint is a constraint-in-advance
====
The licensing constraint above is the frozen consequence both the calculus and its
proof harness must respect: any fractional split / rejoin / routing is licensed
*only* on the cancellative core stem:[\mathcal{C}] (tier 1), is order-sensitive
(F2 / R-2026-06-23-01), and is *forbidden* on tiers 2 and 3. The order-sensitivity
attaches to the routing *operation*, not to the internal composition of core
grades: within stem:[\mathcal{C}] the grade product reduces to fidelity addition
(`Delta.Q_add`), which is commutative *and* cancellative, so the core licenses
*both* losslessness and commutative reassembly. It is the operation's general form
— which may move grades between coordinates and tiers — that the order discipline
of R-2026-06-23-01 governs.

This addendum *defines no split / rejoin / routing operator* — none exists in
v0.1. The obligation here *constrains in advance* any such operator a future
revision may introduce; the operator itself is listed as OPEN-A in §8.
====

== R-2026-06-23-04 — partition correction: `Dropped` and `Total` are *honest*

[cols="1,4",options="header"]
|===
| ID | Correction (normative, dated for the decision-log)

| *R-2026-06-23-04* | `Dropped` and `Total` *MUST* be classified as *honest*, not
deceptive. `Dropped` is *honest withholding* — a field withheld entirely, the
clean omission whose *deceptive dual* is the distinct fate `Falsified`. `Total`
is the *honest* stem:[\infty] — total quantified loss — whose deceptive
counterpart is the fidelity bottom `Unknown` (loss of *unknown* amount).
Documentation, taxonomy tables, and tooling *MUST NOT* enumerate `Dropped` or
`Total` in the deceptive tier (tier 3 of R-2026-06-23-03).
|===

This correction is recorded explicitly because it *contradicts informal
phrasings* that treat any absorbing or bottom-like element as deceptive. Both
`Dropped` and `Total` are *absorbing* in their coordinate yet *truthful*:
`Dropped` is left-absorbing on `Fate` (`Fate.dropped_absorbing`) while remaining
an honest omission; `Total` absorbs finite fidelity (`Delta.total_add_Q`) and is
itself absorbed only by the deceptive `Unknown` (`Delta.total_add_unknown`),
yet asserts no falsehood. The distinction *absorbing-but-honest* versus
*absorbing-and-deceptive* is the whole reason the boundary is three-tier
(R-2026-06-23-03): it is *precisely* because `Dropped`, `Total`, `Severed`, and
`Predicated` are honest-yet-non-cancellative that "cancellation fails stem:[\iff]
deception" is *false*.

[NOTE]
====
*Carrier reconciliation.* The proved `Fate` coordinate has *five* inhabitants —
it includes `Falsified`, the deceptive fate-bottom dual to honest `Dropped`. v0.1
§3.1's fate table lists only *four* (`Present`, `Attenuated(δ)`, `Predicated(P)`,
`Dropped`) and predates `Falsified`. This addendum records `Falsified` as
established by the carrier; whether v0.1 §3.1 should be amended to introduce it is
a documentation-reconciliation decision for the merge author, logged as OPEN-B
(§8). Nothing here edits v0.1 §3.1.
====

== Relationship to the v0.1 obligations

*R-2026-06-23-03 constrains, and does not replace, L5.* v0.1's *L5* ("conflation
infectiousness": once the merge coordinate is `Conflated`, no composition restores
`Fused` or `Single`) stands unchanged. R-2026-06-23-03 makes L5's irreversibility
*precise* — it is *left-absorption of the deceptive bottom* — and *generalises* it
from the merge coordinate alone to *every* coordinate's deceptive bottom
(`Falsified`, `Misbound`, `Conflated`, `Unknown`), with the full-carrier
consequence `grade_not_leftCancel`. Read together: v0.1's L5 is the merge-coordinate
instance of the general left-absorption fact that R-2026-06-23-03 records.

*L1–L4 are reinforced, not altered.* R-2026-06-23-01 records the L1–L2 monoid at
full strength and adds the order discipline (non-commutativity) that L1's
associativity alone does not capture. L3 (bottom-absorption) and L4 (monotonicity)
are consistent with, and presupposed by, the tier structure of R-2026-06-23-03.

*O1 is untouched and remains open.* v0.1's *O1* — that the seven effects are
*exhaustive* — is a separate completeness question and is *not* addressed,
advanced, or closed here. The algebraic facts recorded in this addendum concern
the *structure of the grade monoid*, not the *enumeration of the effects that
populate it*; the two are orthogonal. O2–O4 are likewise untouched.

== OPEN, for author review

The following surfaced while authoring and are *genuine gaps* that F1–F4 and the
partition correction do *not* settle. In line with the brief, they are *recorded,
not filled*.

* *OPEN-A — the split / rejoin / routing operator is referenced but undefined.*
  R-2026-06-23-03 (and the frozen consequence it records) governs the
  cancellability of *fractional split / rejoin / routing*, yet no such operator is
  defined in v0.1 or in the grade-boundary harness. The obligation constrains an
  operation that does not yet exist. Whether to introduce it — and if so, with what
  typing rule — is for author review; until then R-2026-06-23-03 stands as a
  *constraint-in-advance*.

* *OPEN-B — `Falsified` is absent from v0.1 §3.1.* The proved `Fate` coordinate is
  five-ary, but v0.1 §3.1's fate table lists four inhabitants and does not mention
  `Falsified` (the deceptive dual of `Dropped`). This addendum records `Falsified`
  as carrier-established; whether the eventual v0.2 should amend v0.1 §3.1 to
  introduce it normatively — and how that interacts with the v0.1 §6.1
  conflation-as-fault story
  for the *fate* coordinate — is a documentation-reconciliation decision, not one
  fixed by F1–F4.

* *OPEN-C — tier-2 membership is recorded, not exhaustively proved.* The harness
  proves *representative* honest-but-lossy elements non-cancellative (`Predicated`,
  `Severed`, `Total`). That `Withheld` and `Fused` also sit in tier 2 (honest,
  non-cancellative) is asserted here from the carrier's order structure but is not
  individually witnessed by a named theorem in the current harness. Whether to add
  those witnesses, or to prove tier-2 membership uniformly, is for author review.
