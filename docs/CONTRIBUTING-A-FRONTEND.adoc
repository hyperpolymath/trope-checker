= Targeting the Trope IR — a guide for new front-ends
:toc: macro
:sectnums:

This is the document that makes "portable" real: how a _new_ front-end (a
language, or a static analyser for an existing one) targets the Trope IR so the
same checker trope-checks it. Haec is the first front-end; it is not privileged.

toc::[]

== 1. Emit the IR, not a verdict

Your front-end *lowers* its program to a Trope IR document (`schemas/trope-ir.schema.json`)
and hands it to the checker. You do not decide acceptability; the checker does,
against each consumer's declared use-model floor. Validate your output:
`python3 src/checker/validate_schema.py` (after adding your fixtures) and run it
through `src/checker/tropecheck.py`.

== 2. The grade vocabulary you may emit (prevent profile)

Per edge, a grade `Σ(fate). Bond(fate.bearer) × Merge`:

* *fate* per field: `Present`, `Attenuated{delta}` (`delta` ∈ ℕ or `"inf"`/`"top"`),
  `Predicated{predicate}` (quality field only), `Dropped`.
* *bond*: `Intact` (bearer present) or `Withheld`/`Severed` (bearer absent).
* *merge*: `Single`, or `Fused{tau}` with a REQUIRED provenance tag.

You may NOT emit a deceptive grade (`Falsified`/`Misbound`/`Conflated`) or an
untagged merge: under the prevent profile these are malformed and the validator
rejects them, naming the coordinate. That rejection _is_ the conflation/deception
detector at your lowering boundary (HC-3, R-2026-06-22).

== 3. Your obligation: lowering correctness (O2)

The checker's soundness is *relative to faithful lowering*. It cannot verify that a
construct you lower to (say) a `collapse` _is_ one, nor that a value you mark
faithful is not in fact false. *Proving each construct's lowering faithful is YOUR
obligation*, discharged per construct in your repository — not something the
checker provides. State it per construct even before the proof exists (see Haec's
`design/elaboration.adoc` for the pattern).

== 4. The conformance contract

To claim conformance, your toolchain MUST reproduce, for every pair in
`tests/conformance/cases.json`, the same verdict (`p-sufficient`/`p-insufficient`)
and, where given, the same witness edge; and every document in
`tests/conformance/schema-invalid/` MUST be rejected at the named coordinate.
"Portable" means _means-the-same-everywhere_, enforced by this suite — not merely
_runs-everywhere_. Add your own fixtures alongside; do not weaken existing ones.

== 5. What you get back

A verdict object: `{ "verdict": "p-sufficient" }`, or
`{ "verdict": "p-insufficient", "failing_coordinates": [...], "witness": "<edge id>",
"witness_coordinate": "<coord>" }`. The witness is the first edge that pushed the
running grade below the floor — the edge to repair.
