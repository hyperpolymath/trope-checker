== Summary

*Net soundness-relevant escape hatches in this repository: 0.*

The trusted-base scanner reports Idris2 `believe_me` markers (`Idr-blv=6`). On
inspection, *every* one of those matches is a *convention comment* in a proof-file
header that *names the construct in order to forbid it* — not a use of it. No proof
in `verification/proofs/` invokes `believe_me`, `assert_total`, `idris_crash`, a
top-level `partial`, `unsafePerformIO`, or `unsafeCoerce`; the Lean and Agda
developments are likewise `sorry`-free / zero-`postulate` (audited separately —
`verification/proofs/INTEGRATION-REPORT.adoc`). The matches are therefore *false
positives* of a substring scanner that is not comment-aware (the same class of
false positive recorded for the local `proof-scan-dangerous` recipe in the
integration report).

== Standing position

* No escape hatch is admitted into the trusted base. The verified Idris2 core, the
  Lean grade-algebra ground truth, and the Agda metatheory are all axiom-clean for
  their claimed lemmas.
* The convention comments are retained deliberately: they document the prohibition
  at the point of work. The *root* fix for the scanner mismatch is to make
  `check-trusted-base.sh` comment-aware upstream in `hyperpolymath/standards`
  (solutions-at-source); this ledger records the local ground truth in the
  meantime.
* Should a genuine escape hatch ever be introduced, it MUST be added here under
  class (a), (b), or (c) with its justification before the proof is accepted.
